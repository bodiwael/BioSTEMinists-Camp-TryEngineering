A day inside the camp
Biosignals
Why this camp exists

Healthcare is the doorway. Engineering is the destination.

We pair themes that already resonate with girls — care, empathy, helping people — with rigorous technical skill-building in programming, electronics, and AI.

Vision

Women leading health tech

A future where young women design inclusive, human-centered healthcare solutions — not just use the technology, but build it.

Mission

From challenge to mastery

Real medical needs become accessible entry points into biomedical engineering, AI, IoT, and VR — guided by women mentors in STEM.

Method

Build, test, exhibit

Every camper finishes with a working device — and the confidence of demoing it live to judges, families, and healthcare professionals.

Two tracks, one mission

Built for where each camper is starting from.

Age-appropriate, but never watered down — both tracks ship a real, working biomedical project by Day 3.

Ages 5–12
Junior Innovators

Biosignals you can see, hear, and build.

  • Arduino-based heart rate monitors with LED feedback
  • Smart health guardians with temperature alert sensors
  • Computer vision image-sorting activities
  • VR health education with Google Cardboard
  • Mini hackathon & interactive health tech fair on Day 3
Ages 12–18
Young Researchers

Real medical problems, real engineering.

  • EEG-based epileptic seizure detection
  • Freezing-of-Gait prediction for Parkinson's patients
  • IoT heart-attack prediction with ECG & cloud connectivity
  • AI pneumonia detection using CNNs on X-ray imaging
  • VR medical rehabilitation & training simulations
Three days, one arc

Each day builds on the last.

Mornings are technical sessions; afternoons are hands-on build time — culminating in a public hackathon exhibition.
